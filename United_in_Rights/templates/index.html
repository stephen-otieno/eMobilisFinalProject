About Us

United in Rights is an independent, voluntary and non-government organisation that aims at advocating for equality in the society by fighting the uprising gender based violence. The cases of gender based violence have been on an upward trajectory for some decades now. So many methods have been implemented to fight this monster but still the results are not pleasant. Unlike other organisations, at UR we advocate for the rights of everyone in the society; the old, the young, boys and girls, children and adults. We believe in inclusivity and equity in the society.

Common Gender Based Violence Abuses



In most of the gender based violence cases reported, ladies are the ones who are being humiliated. Some of the humiliations and abuses inflicted on females include:

  • Sexual Assault
  • Forced Female Genital Mutilation
  • Domestic Abuse
  • Education Inequality
  • Cyberbullying




Though not common, male species are also subjected to abuse by their partners at home, bosses at work or even friends at their meeting places and any random place. Many organisations and religious groups are known for actively fighting Gender Based Violence in favour of the female species, with the assumption that the male species can stand for themselves. The sad truth is that even the males are vulnerable and are being abused. Some of the abuses inflicted on male species are:

  • Domestic violence
  • Cyberbullying
  • Workplace hazards and discrimination.
  • Sexual Assault for instance rape
  • Mandatory military service in some countries
  • Health neglect
  • Gender Stereotyping

Frequently Asked Questions

Some of the frequently asked questions by our clients and victims include:

1. What is Gender Based Violence (GBV)?

GBV refers to harmful acts directed at individuals based on their gender. It includes physical, sexual, emotional, psychological, and economic abuse, among others.

2. Who is most affected by GBV??

While GBV can affect anyone, it impacts women, girls, and marginalized groups in most cases. Anyone can be victimised regardless of your age, gender or background.

3. What should I do if I’m experiencing GBV?

Reach out to us via our hotline, visit our center, or contact local authorities. Prioritize your safety and seek support. We are only a phone call away.

4. Will my information be kept confidential?

Yes, your privacy is our priority. We follow strict confidentiality policies.

5. What are the charges for Victim treatment after being abused?

Our services are absolutely free of charge to anyone who seeks our help. United in Rights is a voluntary and independent organization
